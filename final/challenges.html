5 non-fiction

Nonfiction 1

Memoir of a queer Muslim. Really interesting and insighful. I think it's useful for anyone struggling with reconciling their faith with identity.

Nonfiction 2

Being from Chicago, I thought it was important to read about the history of the city that often goes ignored.

Nonfiction 3

Collection of essays from disability rights activists. This is a great introduction to the topic. I like that intersectionality was a consistent throughline in this collection. It's always important to read from diverse topics and POVs.

Nonfiction 4

This was a difficult read considering the violence involved. I appreciated how the author humanized both sides without condoning the attack. This book covers a wide range of topics, from transphobia to incarceration of youth.

Nonfiction 5

As an avid digital reader, I was curious to see what this book had to say. While I had some issues with this book, I found it overall helpful. It definitely inspired me to incorporate more print books in my reading.

Nonfiction 6 (Extra)

This is a book that I think everyone should read. If you care about liberal ideals, you need to use your voice to support Palestinian rights. It was really effective in pointing out the double standard in conversations surrounding the topic.

Nonfiction 7 (Extra)

One of my favorite books of the year. It talked about complex scientific breakthroughs in a way that was digestible. I have such an immense appreciation for so many advancements that I've taken forgranted before.

Nonfiction 8 (Extra)

A quick book for all the neuroscience girlies. A good review of the basics.

Nonfiction 9 (Extra)

After dealing with some mental health issues, I was inspired to read this. I think it is a great book that combats toxic positivity.

Nonfiction 10 (Extra)

Fun fact: I was a big insect girlie as a child. It was nice going back to my roots in a sense. It effectively summarized a little-known, yet pressing issue today.

Nonfiction 11 (Extra)

A celebrity memoir that is actually well written. Given the subject matter, it is difficult to read at times. But overall a really well done book. If you grew up with ICarly like me, I would recommend this one for sure.

June
Pride Readathon

LGBTQ+ Main Character

This book has at least 10 main characters. So while not an LGBTQ+ story per say, it works for this challenge.

LGBTQ+ Author

Carmen Maria Machado is a masterful writer who often focuses on LGBTQ+ and feminist issues. I would highly recommend Machado's In the Dream House as well.

LGBTQ+ Nonfiction

This book is about a true hate crime against a trans kid on the bus. It was difficult to read knowing that it was real and that it happened in Oakland. The book did a good job of humanizing everyone involved without condoning the crime.

Multiple LGBTQ+ Identities

I love this book because it shows the diversity of queerness. It portrayed the experience of being in a 'straght' friend group only for everyone to come out. (Happened to me. Twice.). I also love that it isn't hyperfocused on romance.

Different to you.

This book features two lesbians (an identity I don't share). It was written by a wife duo, and everything was so lovely. I liked that it explored deeper themes in a way that didn't feel performative, yet still remained true to its romcom roots.
